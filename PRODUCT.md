# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

GreenCabins speaks first to city couples planning a quiet weekend away from noise, schedules, and over-designed hotel experiences. They arrive in an exploratory mood, looking for a place that feels personal, calm, and trustworthy before they choose dates or ask about availability.

## Product Purpose

GreenCabins helps people discover a very small collection of handpicked Swedish forest cabins. Success means visitors quickly understand the curatorial promise, feel the atmosphere of the stays, and move naturally from the homepage into a cabin detail or contact path without the site feeling like a mass-market booking engine.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, curated, sincere. The voice should feel human and specific: slow-travel confidence, close observation, and practical warmth. It should make the forest feel reachable without turning it into wellness copy or luxury resort theatre.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic booking marketplace grids, Airbnb-style card repetition, luxury hotel polish, resort language, eco cliches, leaf badges, vague sustainability claims, and overly dark dramatic interfaces that make the site hard to read. GreenCabins should not look like a catalogue trying to maximize inventory.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with atmosphere before inventory. Let the visitor feel the stay before asking them to compare cabins.
2. Keep the collection small on purpose. Scarcity is part of the trust signal, not a limitation to hide.
3. Make every cabin feel personally known. Use concrete details, honest availability, and careful pacing.
4. Prefer quiet confidence over sales pressure. Calls to action should feel like invitations, not conversion machinery.
5. Design for recovery. The interface should reduce noise, preserve legibility, and make decisions feel unhurried.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use a WCAG AA baseline for contrast, focus visibility, keyboard navigation, semantic landmarks, and descriptive link text. Respect reduced-motion preferences and avoid motion that blocks reading or navigation. Keep copy plain, specific, and readable for visitors who may be browsing quickly on mobile.
